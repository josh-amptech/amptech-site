# [Story]: Address homepage issues surfaced in hallway usability testing

## Story (Yellow)

As a qualified visitor landing on the homepage from search or referral, I want the page to clearly show whether AmpTech is a fit for my project and why I should trust the team, so that I can decide whether to book a discovery call without hesitation.

## Rules (Blue) - Acceptance Criteria

- The homepage includes concrete proof near the top of the page, such as case studies, client evidence, screenshots, outcomes, or trust signals.
- The homepage makes AmpTech's target audience and project fit more explicit.
- The homepage reduces ambiguity for qualified founder and product buyers while also clarifying whether broader website and business buyers are in scope.
- The homepage includes examples or messaging that map to the intended buyer segments.
- The updated page preserves the existing strong CTA visibility and clear next-step flow.
- The updated page can be re-tested with the same hallway survey for before-and-after comparison.

## Examples (Green) - Concrete Scenarios

**Rule: proof is visible**
- Given a first-time visitor lands on the homepage, when they scan the first half of the page, then they see concrete evidence that AmpTech has delivered real work.
- Given a skeptical referral visitor compares AmpTech against other agencies, when they review the homepage, then they can find proof beyond general claims of experience.

**Rule: project fit is explicit**
- Given a founder with an app or MVP idea lands on the homepage, when they read the hero and supporting sections, then they can tell the service is designed for that kind of project.
- Given a small-business visitor looking for a basic website refresh lands on the homepage, when they review the offer, then they can quickly tell whether that work is in scope.

**Rule: messaging aligns to intended audience**
- Given the business wants founder-first positioning, when visitors read the homepage, then the messaging consistently reinforces that focus and does not imply a broader service set accidentally.
- Given the business wants broader buyer coverage, when non-founder visitors review the homepage, then they can find examples and language relevant to their use case.

**Rule: CTA clarity remains intact**
- Given a qualified visitor decides they are interested, when they finish scanning the homepage, then the next step to book a discovery call is still obvious and low-friction.

**Rule: measurable follow-up is possible**
- Given the updated homepage ships, when the same hallway survey is run again, then the team can compare clarity, relevance, trust, and booking intent against the 2026-03-23 baseline.

## Questions (Red) - Open Unknowns

- Is the homepage meant to stay tightly focused on founders and product buyers, or should it also convert broader website-service traffic?
- What proof assets are available right now: logos, screenshots, testimonials, case studies, measurable results, or references?
- Are there existing client examples that can be published publicly?
- Should broader audiences be handled on the homepage itself or on dedicated landing pages?

## Out of Scope

- Rewriting the entire site information architecture
- Building new service lines that AmpTech does not actually offer
- Full case-study page implementation unless separately scoped
- SEO strategy beyond the homepage positioning and messaging implications
